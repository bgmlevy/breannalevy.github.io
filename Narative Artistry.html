My mom always reminds me of my “someday” where everything I dream of unfolds before my eyes. Dreaming big became not just a goal but a way of life for me. I embodied the concept that nothing could hold me back from the dream life I am building for myself.

Eyes overlooking represent my mother; she knows that the challenges I encounter are just another bridge I have to cross. The design illustrates how these obstacles are going to fade. The direction the subject is heading towards strays from the past. The dark and dramatic composition has a profound message that prompts viewers to consider the power that comes from overcoming misfortune and embracing the way ahead with renewed clarity and strength.

Philosophy was my favourite subject in high school. It showed me the different perspectives of life and how to mediate my life. This image, filled in symmetry and balance, reflects the delicate balance sought in life—directed by two profound philosophical ideologies: happiness and the soul—within the captivating halls of RH Toronto.

 

An eternal human goal, the pursuit of pleasure serves as a compass that guides our decisions, deeds, and relationships, weaving a harmonious tale like to the well-balanced edifice it portrays. At the same time, the idea of the soul gives our life more depth by entwining with our feelings and goals and acting as the invisible thread that binds us to the deepest facets of mankind. The interaction of happiness and the soul creates a visual metaphor in the passageways of life, a dance of harmony between the search for joy and the deepest core of our existence.

 

 

 

 

 

Breanna Levy Life Scale (January 2023)
Photography and Digital Art | Adobe Illustrator
A collection of significant pieces that breathed life into art.

The time was going by slow. I was admitted into hospital mid October in 2021 where I faced the battle of my eating disorder. I felt at my lowest, I feel like I failed caring for my well-being. “If this wasn’t caught sooner, we wouldn’t know how long you’d live for,” was what the doctors told me. There was no escape from the IV, heart monitors, daily blood-tests, or the hospital walls.

 

The piece's main subject is a little girl who is seen falling from an hourglass, a moving representation of the unstoppable passage of time, and the girl's fall represents her battle against the forces holding her captive in this space of time. The sensation of suspended animation is emphasized by the sand grains' motionless fall, which creates a stagnant environment.

 

She expresses a profound feeling of helplessness and hopelessness through her posture and disposition. Her arms are spread, reaching desperately for an escape that is still out of reach, highlighting the pointlessness of her attempts to escape the confines of time. The observer is nearly made to feel as though time is pushing the girl irresistibly downward with a gravitational force.
