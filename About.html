I am presently pursuing a Global Business and Digital Arts degree at the University of Waterloo, focusing on the intersection of digital design and business relations. As an undergraduate student, I am committed to expanding my expertise in this field.

 

My passion for merging creativity and business drives me to constantly evolve and push the boundaries of my skill set. I am dedicated to leveraging my education and practical knowledge to contribute to innovative projects and deliver exceptional results.

 

When I’m not designing, you can find me... sleeping. I’m always doing something creative.

Art serves as a therapeutic journey, allowing me to visualize and relinquish the intensity of feelings that words cannot explain.

 

I not only express but liberate myself from the echoes of past traumas that once resided within my mind. Art is the silent language where emotions take tangible form, providing a sanctuary for healing and self-discovery.

 

High school was not the best four years of my life like they say in the movies. There were many events during those four years where I felt alone. I would come home crying to my mom most days about how much I hated school and felt like I was never appreciated for the person I am.

 

I lost myself within the first three years, yes I love art but in those first three years it felt like I didn’t have that connection anymore.

Was what a guidance counsellor told me in high school during one of the most traumatic experiences that had ever occurred. In these moments I never thought I’d make it over the bridge of my own challenges. I turned to art when I felt alone, I let every paint stroke, stutter, pencil marks, showcase my stories.

 

As I continue to develop and learn about life, that is what I abide by and live by. I can now look back on my artwork with confidence and smile. Despite our differences, we managed to come together, with the younger me pleading for the older me to find her sanctuary. She would be proud with our progress and the exquisite works of art we are able to produce by finding light in the shadows.

I made it. I graduated high school, a moment in my life where I once thought I’d never see. By this time I was committed to continue my art journey at the University of Waterloo for Global Business and Digital Arts. My art has taken me to build my dream life and it continues to fulfilling my life in big and small ways. I am becoming the woman little Breanna always dreamed of being.

 

With all my personal success, I owe it to my family. The strong women who show me to live life to the fullest. My older and younger sister constantly remind me to never forget my roots. Their constant encouragement empowers me to embrace creativity fearlessly in my art, urging me to let loose and take risks, shaping my artistic journey with inspiration and courage. To the strongest woman I know, my mother. She is resilient and will motivate me to take on obstacles head-on and have faith in my own talents. She reminds me that I am capable of greatness and gives me the courage to pursue my dreams with unending love. Her strong presence continues to be an inspiration of hope, influencing my path to achieving my greatest goals.

 

In the first month of grade 12 I was admitted to the hospital for a month and some change. This was the downfall yet the spark to my artistic journey to have a fresh start. When I was in the hospital I was restricted by the four walls around me with only my mom, nurses, and my sisters I could only see virtually.

 

I had my iPad, laptop, and sketchbook to keep me from loosing my sanity. That month I have never drawn or created digital art more. I’d say more than I have done compared to thus far in university. I made hard times easy having art with me. Days would go by in the hospital and I started to translate that into real-life. This became a valuable perspective I carry on in my day-to-day life as I appreciated the art around me. Whether that was outside, literature in books, or the small drawing I made on a napkin.
